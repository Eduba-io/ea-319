What is visible from the outside

The certification curriculum is already an ICM-shaped problem.

VMT's signal is clear from the outside. 2017 founding, three co-founders with FAU Tech Runway roots, a certification process that puts every mentor through eight hours of training and twenty hours of supervised team mentoring, 200+ certified volunteers, and chapters running at thirteen universities plus corporate programs at Office Depot, Goldman Sachs, Target, and Macy's. The mission is free mentorship for founders.

The quiet operational reality is that the certification curriculum, the chapter playbook, and the mentor-session conventions are the load-bearing assets, and they travel by goodwill and institutional habit rather than by a structured, versioned artifact.

A Kentucky mentor and a Delray mentor should produce the same experience. A structured methodology file is what keeps that true at thirty chapters.
The frame we would bring

Computational orchestration. Which layer each piece of the work belongs on.

Most organizations look at their operations and ask where AI can help. That is usually the wrong first question. A more useful one is: which parts of what we do are reproducible rules, which parts need human judgment, and which parts are actually waiting for a different kind of structure before either AI or humans can scale them. Eduba calls this computational orchestration.

For VMT, the answer almost certainly is not a chatbot for founders. It is more likely a structured methodology file that every chapter runs against, a shared intake and session-capture layer so mentor time stops leaking into admin, and a reporting layer that lets sponsor-specific views render from one clean source.

About sixty percent of that is traditional software, thirty percent is rule-based logic, and ten percent is where language models belong. Getting the layers right is the whole game.

The relevant case study

Edinburgh and UKICER: methodology that stands up to peer review.

Jake collaborated with the University of Edinburgh and the UK Institute of Coding Education Research on academic-grade methodology work. That partnership was not about selling a tool. It was about co-authoring the rigor that lets a method stand up to peer review.

VMT's certification process is in the same category of artifact: something that needs to travel between sites with fidelity and hold up to scrutiny from sponsors, universities, and regulators. The Edinburgh work proves Eduba treats methodology as a first-class product.

The relevant paper

Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS.

The published version of the approach lives at github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-. Folder structure as agent architecture. The same idea applies, in plain form, to a certification curriculum that needs to stay faithful across chapters.

Jake also built an online community to 22,000 members in five weeks. One line, nothing to click. Evidence of distribution instinct when it matters.
